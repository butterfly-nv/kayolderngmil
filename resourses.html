What is Media and Information Literacy?

The ability to access, examine, assess, and produce information in a variety of formats across many media platforms is known as media and information literacy, or MIL. It enables people to interact with media information critically, comprehend how media affects society, and make wise decisions in the digital age. MIL encourages the moral and responsible use of information, assisting individuals in becoming more knowledgeable consumers, citizens, and content producers.

Do you know what fake news is?

The term "fake news" describes material that is purposefully incorrect or deceptive, frequently with the objective of tricking people. It is usually disseminated to sway public opinion, create confusion, or make money. It can take many different forms, including made-up tales, photoshopped images, or films. Fake news is dangerous and untrustworthy since it lacks dependable sources and verification, in contrast to true news.

What you should do

  • Post reliable and verifiable information, such as that released by officially recognized institutions or agencies, or the same information obtained from many sources.
  • Verify the facts or message before posting or spreading it, as the erroneous message might hurt you and others.
  • Before uploading, posting, and sharing someone else's images or videos online, think about the contents and implications that you and others will face because they are extremely difficult to manage or erase once they are online. You should ask yourself, "If you were them, would you want to be exposed online? Is there a photo or video you want your parents or children to see? Would you like your supervisor or employer to view it?"
  • Respect the rights of children, vulnerable groups, and women by avoiding uploading or distributing photographs, videos, audiotapes, or articles that may violate their privacy, values, or dignity.
  • Secure the intellectual property of your photos, videos, text, and audio safely online. To safeguard your online property, you must be familiar with intellectual property regulations and copyright information.
  • Before sharing something to social media, you should ask necessary questions such as: Is it true? Is it important? Is it really necessary?

What you should not do

  • Do not use the internet to disseminate material that undermines people's dignity and reputation.
  • Do not promote phony photos, bogus videos, or voicemail messages, since they harm people's reputations and may result in legal action.
  • Sending, posting, or sharing threatening comments, images, or videos that threaten or intimidate people is unethical and may result in criminal penalties against the culprit.
  • Do not post anything that might make the public confused, angry, or scared.
  • Do not share unsubstantiated, inaccurate, or misinformed news that leads to racist attitudes, religious conflict, or attacking any individual in society.
  • Do not publish anything that diminishes or destroys cultural values or your country's dignity.
  • Don't believe everything you see or hear on the internet since it's not necessarily real and truthful.
  • Do not publish information on the internet that relates to drugs, alcohol, gambling and violence as it is a bad example for society, especially for young people.
