About misinformation

There are seven types of misinformation:

1. Satire and parody: are amusing means of addressing the moment all over the world. Satire or parody utilizes irony, comedy, and sarcasm. This implies they can occasionally trick people into believing they are honest.

2. False connections: occur when headlines do not accurately reflect what is included in an article or when an image's caption is deceptive.

3. Misleading content: uses real facts in a way that leads you to erroneous conclusions. It may present an opinion as truth or provide you with a set of facts intended to lead you to a false conclusion.

4. False context: occurs when actual information is presented but the information around it seems to be true. Real videos with deceptive descriptions and imagery that actually portray something different are simple methods for deceiving people online.

5. Imposter content: pretends to be something it is not. Videos, photos, and even news items may all be faked online. Like the impostor in Super Sus, not everything online is what it appears to be.

6. Manipulated content: relies on changed photos, false video edits, or other modified content to trick you into thinking something that is wrong.

7. Fabricated content: is simply fabricated in order to fool you and affect your emotions. Deep fabricate and AI technologies can be used to fabricate whole videos.
